SpeakersJS

speakers, prospective speakers, mentors, & community members collaborating on Javascript talks

Mission

By connecting speakers and prospective speakers with mentors and members of the community to ideate and collaborate, we hope to:

  • Help people deliver better talks about Javascript
  • Enable a more diverse group of people to become speakers
  • Build stronger communities

Contributing

Organizers

To add your community, please open a pull request.

We'll add your community to this page and create a new repository for your organization under the speakersjs organization. You will be given admin access to that new repository.

Speakers and Prospective Speakers

To connect with your community, open an issue in your community's repository.

Make your issue title clear, and include in your issue description all the information you can think of to help your community help you.

Examples:

  • If you're looking for help choosing a topic, you could open an issue with a few topic ideas you're considering.
  • If you're looking for help responding to a call for proposals, you could post a draft of your response and ask for feedback.
  • If you're looking for help finishing a talk, you could open an issue with a link to an outline or Google doc with what you've got so far.
  • If you're looking for feedback on a finished talk before you deliver it, you could open an issue with a link to a webcast recording of your presentation.

Also, feel free to check out other communities. Maybe you can find some ideas or help other speakers!

Mentors and Community Members

To help your community have awesome talks from a diverse set of speakers, jump into your community's repository and give some feedback.

Also, feel free to contribute to other communities!

No matter how you contribute, remember: be respectful and kind. :)

Examples:

  • When speakers ask for help choosing a topic, give honest, constructive feedback about which suggested topics you like. Give reasons!
  • If you have experience submitting responses to a call for proposals, help others who are seeking help doing the same.
  • When speakers are looking for help finishing a talk, share your knowledge or identify questions you've always had about the topic they're speaking about.
  • When speakers are looking for feedback on a finished talk, watch it. Think about whether the core message is clear, and whether the talk is focused or needs to be tightened up.
